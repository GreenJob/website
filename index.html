Western Ghats and Water Security

Studying the effects of agroforestry on water security in peninsular India.

Western Ghats is a key biodiversity hotspot in the world, and of crucial importance to water security in peninsular India. The rainfall patterns feed the major rivers Cauvery, Krishna, and others supporting water provisioning in Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Goa, and Gujarat. In this project, we focus on Karnataka as a starting point to evaluate the role of forests in the water yield potential in the water sheds. This is explored further by the role of agroforestry in supporting primary dense forests.
